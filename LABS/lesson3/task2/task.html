To familiarize yourself with boolean expressions, fix the following expressions by changing the '?'s so that each of the print statements prints out True.

Recall that in Python the operators for AND, OR and NOT are the keywords 'and' 'or' and 'not'.

That is to say... if you wanted to check 'if a and b' were true and do something if they were True, you would write:
and if a and b:
      do something
